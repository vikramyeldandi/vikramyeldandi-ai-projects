01 — Problem & Objective

Why this agent exists

Retail customer service teams field the same questions repeatedly — return windows, store hours, delivery timelines, loyalty points. These are low-complexity, high-volume queries that don't need a human agent. But a generic AI left unconstrained will hallucinate policy details, discuss competitors, or give advice it shouldn't.

The objective was to build a grounded, guardrailed agent that answers only from a verified policy document — and knows exactly what to do when a question falls outside its scope.

The Problem

Generic AI answers customer questions with general knowledge — not your actual policy. This creates inconsistency, liability, and trust erosion.

The Objective

An agent that answers only from the policy document, gracefully handles out-of-scope questions, and escalates sensitive issues like fraud.

03 — System Prompt & Grounding Design

How the agent is grounded

The entire policy document is embedded directly into the system prompt. The agent is instructed to treat this as its only source of truth — no general knowledge, no inference beyond what's written. This is a context window grounding pattern: simple, auditable, and effective for documents under ~10,000 tokens.

System Prompt Structure 
// Identity & Role
You are the Luminary Home Customer Service Assistant
— a helpful, friendly, and professional AI agent.

// Grounding Instruction
YOUR JOB: Answer customer questions using ONLY the
POLICY DOCUMENT below. Do not use general knowledge.

// Strict Rules (Guardrails)
STRICT RULES:
1. Only answer from the policy document.
   If not covered → direct to [email protected]
2. Never make up information.
3. Never discuss competitor brands.
4. Never give legal, financial, or medical advice.
5. Acknowledge frustration before answering.
6. For fraud or account security → tell customer
   to call immediately. Do not resolve.

// Tone & Format
FORMAT: 3-5 sentences max. Bullets for multi-part answers.
Always end with "Is there anything else I can help you with?"

// Policy Document (Source of Truth)
POLICY DOCUMENT:
RETURNS: Standard 30 days, unused, original packaging...
STORE HOURS: Mon-Sat 9am-8pm. Sunday 10am-6pm...
REWARDS: 1 point per $1 spent. 100 points = $1 credit...

The policy document is authored once by the business and injected at runtime. Updating policy requires only updating this document — not retraining or fine-tuning the model.

04 — Guardrails & Edge Cases

What happens when things go wrong

Guardrails are where most FAQ bots fail. The system prompt defines explicit rules for six categories of edge cases — each tested against real-world customer inputs.

"I burned half a candle — can I return it?"
→
Politely declines — burned candles are non-returnable per policy
"What are IKEA's return hours?"
→
Blocked — competitor brands are never discussed
"Do you offer student discounts?"
→
Redirects to contact — not covered in policy document
"Someone used my account without permission."
→
Escalates immediately — directs to call 0800 123 4567
"Is this a good investment for my home?"
→
Blocked — no financial or legal advice given
